Exchanges and Indexes
Exchanges

The New York Stock Exchange

The NYSE is the world’s largest exchange, averaging $169 billion in trades per day. It has a listing of nearly 1,900 companies and has around 700 traders on the floor per day. On the 17th of May, 1792, the NYSE was created when 24 stock brokers signed the Buttonwood Agreement in New York City. They signed this agreement to bypass auctioneers and commission rates for products like wheat and tobacco. Their first headquarters were in a small coffee house. After 25 years, the NYSE was officially created, under the name ‘The New York Stock & Exchange Board’. They specialized in just bonds and bank stocks, but then started expanding in the early 1800s. Today, the NYSE is a Fortune 500 company and has a 21 room, trading floor located at 21 Wall Street, New York City, New York.


NASDAQ

NASDAQ, or the Nation Association of Securities Dealers Automated Quotations, is the second largest stock exchange in the world. It was founded in 1971 by the National Association of Securities Dealers and became the world’s first electronic stock market. Like the NYSE, NASDAQ is located in New York City and merged regulation committees with the NYSE in 2007. Today, NASDAQ is the world’s largest electronic stock exchange with over 3,000 participating companies and has its own index in the stock market.





Indexes

DOW
The Dow Jones Industrial Average is a conglomeration of the largest 30 companies in America and shows the sum of one share of stock for each company. The DOW is one of the top, if not the top, meter for how the stock market is doing. By referencing changes in the stock market, most people are quoting the DOW.
S&P 500
The Standard and Poor’s 500 is an index of 500 large companies who have their stock listed within the NYSE or NASDAQ. The S&P 500 is often called the best representation of the US stock market at the time, despite the DOW being quoted more often.
Russell
The Russell 2000 is a measure of the bottom 2000 companies of the Russell 3000, which measures the top 3000 largest publicly held companies in the US. The Russell 2000 is the most quoted market in reference to mutual funds and performance of mutual funds.
Wilshire
The Wilshire 5000 is a measure of all actively trading companies in the US (as calculated by capitalization-weighting). Since the Wilshire 5000 contains up to 6,700 companies today, it is also one of the best measures of the stock market performance, like the S&P 500.
